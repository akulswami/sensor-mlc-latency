`git pull` on Jetson aborted because of untracked working-tree files

then `sudo rm -rf` the directory and `git pull` + `git lfs pull`.

**This will happen every session.** The orchestrator (`run_session.py`)
records on the Jetson and scp's to the Asus, but does not clean up
the Jetson copies. Each subsequent session inherits the prior
session's files as untracked, blocking `git pull`.

Two fixes worth considering:
1. Have `run_session.py` `rm` the Jetson copies after a successful
   scp, since Asus + origin are authoritative.
2. Have `imu_logger` write to a path outside the repo, e.g.
   `/tmp/imu_logger/` or `~/.cache/sensor-mlc-latency/`.

Option 1 is the smaller change. Option 2 is more architecturally
correct but touches more files. Either way, this is workflow tax
that's cost ~5 min per session across three sessions; will recur.

### Mount orientation pre-check

Before kicking off the orchestrator, ran the sensor sanity capture
and computed full-session-style orientation means. Compared against
session 1 and session 2:

| Axis | Session 1 | Session 2 | Session 3 (5-sec sanity) |
|---|---|---|---|
| X mean | ~0 | -0.0088 g | -0.0211 g |
| Y mean | ~0 | -0.0554 g | -0.0526 g |
| Z mean | -0.97 | -0.967 | -0.970 |

Y mean nearly identical to session 2 (3 mg delta). X mean shifted
by 12 mg (~15× noise floor). Reading the evidence honestly: the
re-mount was real but produced thinner orientation variance than
session 2's re-mount did vs session 1. Decision: proceed. Spec
says "introduce position/orientation variance" without specifying
magnitude, and the X delta is empirically distinguishable. notes.md
in the session directory documents this caveat explicitly so
future-reader / reviewer can audit.
