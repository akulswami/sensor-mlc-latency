## Soak test (60s idle + 5s load)

To verify jetson_clocks state was stable (not just transiently
applied), ran sudo /usr/bin/jetson_clocks, then waited 60s idle,
ran a brief dd CPU load for 5s, and verified state at each step.

Result: all CPUs held min=max=1728000=cur throughout. Both the
60s idle period AND the brief CPU load did not trigger any
nvpmodel reassertion. **jetson_clocks was stable in this morning's
fresh-rig state in a way it had not been during last night's
session.**

The reason for the difference is unknown. Possible explanations:
(1) The reboot reset some nvpmodel internal state that had been
accumulating across yesterday's runs. (2) The act of explicitly
storing/restoring DVFS state via jetson_clocks --store during
the night may have interacted with nvpmodel poorly. (3) Random
non-determinism. **Not investigated**; the morning's stable state
was sufficient to launch b703.

## The decisive comparison

Both b702 (mlc idle) and b703 (host idle) had jc_eff = 100.0%
throughout. They are apples-to-apples on the energy axis.

| Block | Pipeline | jc_eff | Energy mean | Energy sd | n_eng |
|------:|----------|-------:|------------:|----------:|------:|
| 702   | mlc      | 100.0% | 7014 mW     | 42        | 3554  |
| 703   | host     | 100.0% | 6982 mW     | 49        | 3551  |

**Gap (host - mlc): -32 mW.** Within the v7.5 section 6.3
plus-or-minus 50 mW noise-floor threshold.

Under jetson_clocks-effective measurement, host and MLC use
statistically indistinguishable energy at idle. The btest +155 mW
finding (which was the basis for v7.5 H4') was an artifact of
comparing measurements taken under free-running DVFS. The host
pipeline's slightly higher CPU utilization caused schedutil to
scale CPUs up more often than for the MLC pipeline; under
locked CPU frequency, this scaling difference disappears and the
energy axis collapses to noise.

**This falsifies v7.5 H4'** ("MLC saves host energy at idle under
vanilla scheduling"). The falsification is empirically clean,
not noise-driven (n>3500 per cell, sd ~45 mW, observed gap is
inside the noise floor).

## What the corrected story means for the paper

The latency story is unchanged from v7.5:

- Host beats MLC bank-switch at idle and under contention.
- Bank-switch protocol overhead (3 I2C transactions per
  measurement) dominates MLC latency.
- Magnitudes are tighter at long-duration jc-effective scale
  but the direction is the same.

The energy story is now SHARPER:

- Under proper measurement (jc-effective, long-duration),
  host and MLC are indistinguishable at idle.
- Razmi and Shojaei 2026 (arXiv:2602.21418) claim BOTH
  low-latency AND energy-efficient on-sensor inference. This
  dataset measures both: the latency claim is refuted, the
  energy claim is quantified at zero (statistically
  indistinguishable from host).
- The paper title can sharpen from "characterization of
  latency and energy" to "neither claim survives proper
  measurement."

## Methodological lessons recorded (additions to yesterday's)

1. **The jc/nvpmodel non-determinism is the most important
   methodological discovery of this project so far.** Without
   per-block jc_eff verification, the energy measurements are
   un-interpretable (the 1858 mW "gap" between b700 and b701
   was an artifact of jc state differing between blocks). All
   future measurements MUST record jc_eff.

2. **A fresh boot may reset accumulated nvpmodel state in a way
   that makes jetson_clocks more stable.** Empirically observed
   this morning, mechanism unknown. Worth investigating but not
   blocking.

3. **The btest +155 mW MLC-saves-energy finding from v7.5 is
   an artifact, not a real effect.** It reflects DVFS scaling
   under free-running governance, not true energy efficiency.
   The btest data should be re-characterized in any paper as
   "free-running DVFS measurement" rather than "energy
   comparison."

4. **One clean apples-to-apples block (b703) was sufficient
   to falsify a hypothesis that was based on 224 samples per
   cell across 18 blocks (btest).** Quality of measurement
   trumps sample size when there's a confounder.
