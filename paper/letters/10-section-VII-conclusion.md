# §VII. Conclusion

We measured wire-level stimulus-to-decision latency for three pipelines on a Jetson Orin Nano + LSM6DSOX edge platform under idle, I²C contention, and CPU stress. Against the commercial intuition that on-sensor inference is faster, the host pipeline achieves 2.1× lower median latency than the MLC bank-switch pipeline at idle (321.7 vs 681.5 µs) and 2.3× lower under I²C contention (574.5 vs 1,325.4 µs); the mlc-binary variant isolates the bank-switch read protocol's cost at ~1,276 µs under contention. The three-transaction I²C read, not the silicon's classification, is the dominant latency contributor. The control results show CPU stress is detectable on energy (+3,420 mW) but null on latency and classifier reliability, while I²C contention is significant on latency but null on reliability — so the safety-critical axis is which resource is contended, not whether the platform is "under stress" in aggregate. We further document a 706.5 ms intrinsic MLC decision cadence imposing a 0–706.5 ms quantization wait on system-level stimulus-to-decision latency. Three originally pre-registered hypotheses — including the headline directional claim — were falsified during the study, each preserved on the public record via Zenodo-timestamped amendments.
