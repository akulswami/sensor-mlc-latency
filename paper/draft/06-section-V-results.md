# §V. Results

*(Target ~900 words. Source material: paper/draft/02-results-table.md; data/processed/confirmatory-2026-05-26/h_prime_results.json, h6_energy_results.json, h7_stability_results.json; paper/figures/figure_1_latency_boxplot.png; paper/figures/figure_2_d0_cadence_histogram.png.)*

The confirmatory campaign collected 4,860 candidate trials across 81 blocks of 300 s; 4,770 trials (98.15%) satisfied the pre-registered inclusion criteria and were retained for latency analysis. All blocks achieved 100% jc-effectiveness under MAXN_SUPER_JC, and all stress-condition verifications passed. Per-cell latency distributions are shown in **Fig. 1**; per-cell summary statistics are reported in **Table I**.

## V.A Confirmatory pre-registered tests

**H1' (host < MLC at idle): SUPPORTED.** Host idle median latency (321.7 µs) is 359 µs lower than MLC bank-switch idle (681.5 µs), a factor of 2.1× speedup for the host pipeline. The Hodges-Lehmann shift estimator gives ΔHL = −359.3 µs with 95% percentile-bootstrap CI [−373.6, −352.5] (10,000 iterations); Mann-Whitney U with two-sided p = 1.87 × 10⁻¹⁷⁰, rejected at the Holm-Bonferroni threshold of α/m = 0.0125 after correction across the {H1', H2', H3', H6', H7'} family.

**H2' (host < MLC under I²C contention): SUPPORTED.** Under three concurrent i2c_hammer processes on bus 7, host median latency rises to 574.5 µs while MLC bank-switch rises to 1325.4 µs. The shift increases in magnitude: ΔHL = −753.2 µs [−760.0, −746.7], 2.3× host advantage. Mann-Whitney p = 2.33 × 10⁻¹⁷⁰, rejected at Holm threshold 0.0167.

**H3' (MLC degrades more than host under contention): SUPPORTED.** The Δ-of-Δ contrast measures how much more the MLC pipeline's median latency rises from idle to contention than the host pipeline's median does. (Δ_MLC − Δ_host) = +391.1 µs with 95% percentile-bootstrap CI [+372.8, +400.0] (10,000 resamples; the contrast is computed by re-using the pre-registered §12 H2 bootstrap function with the MLC and host arguments swapped, an algebraic identity that yields the Δ-of-Δ distribution directly). p = 1.00 × 10⁻⁴, rejected at Holm threshold 0.025. The components: MLC degrades by Δ_MLC = +611.7 µs [+582.7, +639.4] (a +90% increase from 681.5 to 1,325.4 µs), and host degrades by Δ_host = +249.2 µs [+245.9, +253.2] (a +78% increase from 321.7 to 574.5 µs). The MLC pipeline's three-transaction I²C read protocol amplifies the contention penalty.

**H5' (CPU stress null for host latency, ±30 µs equivalence): SUPPORTED.** Under stress-ng saturation, host median latency rises from 321.7 µs (idle) to 345.0 µs (stress). The TOST equivalence test against the pre-registered ±30 µs margin gives median diff +23.3 µs with 90% CI [+22.7, +23.7], strictly contained within [−30, +30]. Equivalence is declared. The host pipeline's latency is insensitive to CPU saturation, as expected for a 208 Hz polling loop that does not contend with stress-ng for CPU time.

**H6' (CPU stress positive for energy, ≥+1000 mW): SUPPORTED.** Mean VDD_IN milliwatts (sampled at 100 ms cadence via INA3221 through `tegrastats`) increases from 5,206 mW (idle) to 8,626 mW (stress), a difference of +3,420 mW with 95% bootstrap CI [+3,410, +3,429]. The Mann-Whitney U test on full-resolution samples returned p below the float64 representable minimum; the same test on 1/10-subsampled data (correcting for the autocorrelation introduced by 100 ms sampling: lag-1 autocorrelation is 0.72 idle, 0.98 stress) also returns p = 0. The +3,420 mW gap exceeds the pre-registered +1,000 mW threshold by more than a factor of three; H6' is rejected at the Holm-Bonferroni threshold of 0.010. The Jetson power axis distinguishes CPU stress unambiguously, even when the latency axis cannot (H5' equivalent).

**H7' (MLC classifier stability degrades under contention): NOT SUPPORTED, direction OPPOSITE to pre-registered prediction.** The fraction of stimulus windows containing exactly one D1 rising edge (the operational definition of classifier stability) is 97.22% (525/540) under MLC idle and 98.89% (534/540) under MLC i2c-contention — a +1.67 percentage-point increase, not the predicted decrease. Fisher's exact test in the pre-registered direction (one-sided alternative='greater', testing OR>1 for idle being more stable) gives p = 0.9874; the two-sided reference test gives p = 0.0755 (marginal, not significant at α=0.05). At the Holm threshold of 0.05 (rank 5 of 5), H7' is the only family member that fails to reject H₀ in the pre-registered direction. H7' is formally falsified in pre-registration v7.10 [REF-PREREG].

The substantive implication: I²C contention adds latency to the MLC pipeline (H2', H3') but does not degrade the silicon's classifier reliability. Host-side classification under contention is slower, not less reliable.

## V.B Multimodal latency distributions

**Fig. 1** reveals previously-unreported multimodal structure in both MLC pipelines. The mlc/idle distribution is visibly bimodal: the mean (866.6 µs) substantially exceeds the median (681.5 µs), and the IQR spans 505.4–1086.8 µs with the p95 reaching 1780.7 µs. The mlc-binary/idle distribution is even more dramatic: median 231.9 µs but three visible modes at approximately 60, 240, and 470 µs. Under i2c-contention and CPU stress, both MLC pipelines collapse to tighter distributions (mlc/contention IQR: 1283.8–1371.5 µs; mlc-binary/contention IQR: 46.9–53.2 µs), suggesting that idle-state variance is the kernel/I²C scheduler exhibiting its full timing-edge variability when not load-pinned by background contention.

This finding is reported as exploratory rather than confirmatory. The mechanistic explanation requires kernel-level instrumentation (ftrace of `i2c-tegra194` and `gpiochip0` event flows) that lies outside the scope of this study. The observation alone — that a "lower mean is better" framing of MLC versus host comparison obscures bimodal substructure visible only at the per-trial timestamping resolution — is a methodological contribution.

## V.C MLC intrinsic decision cadence

**Fig. 2** shows the histogram of inter-trial D0 (INT1) gap times pooled across all MLC and MLC-binary blocks (n = 3,086 gaps, after clipping 44 gaps above 8,831 ms for visibility). The distribution exhibits sharp peaks at integer multiples of T = 706.5 ms, with empty bins between peaks. The dominant peaks at 5×T (3,532 ms; ~1,100 gaps) and 9×T (6,358 ms; ~1,350 gaps) reflect the stimulus protocol's 5 s cycle (one cycle ≈ 5×T; two cycles ≈ 9×T). Secondary peaks at 6×T, 8×T, and 10×T capture the natural cycle-to-cycle variation in the orchestrator's wall-clock pacing.

T = 706.5 ms is one-quarter of the MLC's 75-sample, 26 Hz window period (2.885 s / 4 = 0.721 s; the empirical peak sits at 0.7065 s). This quantization is intrinsic to the MLC silicon: regardless of when the host or stimulus expects a decision, the MLC's classification updates only on its internal 706.5 ms clock. We are not aware of this cadence being documented in ST application notes [REF-AN5259] or in the LSM6DSOX datasheet.

## V.D Trial-level stability and exclusion rates

No cell exceeded the pre-registered 10% trial-exclusion ceiling. The highest exclusion rate was mlc/idle (2.78%), followed by mlc-binary/stress (2.41%) and mlc/i2c-contention (1.48%). Across the 90 excluded trials (1.85% of the 4,860 candidates), the dominant exclusion category was `multiple_d1_in_window` (68/90, 75.6%), which the H7' analysis attributes to the MLC silicon's 706.5 ms cadence interacting with stimulus-window boundaries. The remaining exclusions split evenly between `no_d1_in_window` (11/90, 12.2%) and `multiple_d0_before_d1` (11/90, 12.2%), the latter capturing trials where the MLC fired multiple times before being paired with a stimulus.
