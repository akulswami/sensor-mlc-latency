## Abstract (Variant C, polished with confirmatory data)

The Machine Learning Core (MLC) embedded in the STMicroelectronics LSM6DSOX inertial measurement unit (IMU) is widely cited as a low-latency, low-power alternative to host-side inference, yet end-to-end wire-level latency is rarely measured. Using a Saleae Logic Pro 8 logic analyzer on an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano edge platform, we measured stimulus-to-decision latency for three pipelines — a host-side decision-tree classifier reading raw accelerometer data, the standard MLC bank-switch read protocol, and an MLC binary-fast variant — under idle, I²C bus contention, and CPU stress conditions. The full experimental protocol was pre-registered with twelve externally-timestamped amendments on Zenodo prior to confirmatory data collection (4,770 included trials of 4,860 candidates, 98.15% inclusion, across nine cells, 81 blocks of 300 s each, 100% measurement-condition validity). **The host pipeline exhibits lower median latency than the MLC bank-switch pipeline under all conditions: 321.7 µs vs. 681.5 µs at idle (2.1× faster) and 574.5 µs vs. 1325.4 µs under I²C contention (2.3× faster).** The three-transaction I²C read protocol — not the MLC silicon's classification — is the dominant latency contributor. We additionally document the MLC's previously-unreported 706.5 ms intrinsic decision cadence, observable as quantized inter-edge gaps in the interrupt stream, and identify multimodal latency distributions in both MLC pipelines whose mechanistic origin warrants further kernel-level instrumentation. Pre-registration with external Zenodo DOIs provides an audit trail for methodology evolution. Code, data, and pre-registration: github.com/akulswami/sensor-mlc-latency.
